About Me

Focusing on Interpretable AI in Astrophysics

I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Astronomy at the Xinjiang Astronomical Observatory, Chinese Academy of Sciences (expected graduation: June 2026).


I am broadly interested in data-driven astrophysics, with a particular taste for using data-driven methods to study interesting issues across the field. My work so far has involved multiple areas, including cosmology, high-energy astrophysics, exoplanets, and radio astronomy data processing.

My motivation is rooted in the belief that physics gives rise to data, and that innovative machine learning methods can help uncover the physics hidden within it.
