About Me

Focusing on Interpretable AI in Astrophysics

I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Astronomy at the Xinjiang Astronomical Observatory, Chinese Academy of Sciences (expected graduation: June 2026), working under the supervision of Prof. Na Wang and Prof. Zhiyong Liu.


My primary research interest lies at the intersection of astrophysics and machine learning. Specifically, I am deeply passionate about Interpretable Artificial Intelligence (IAI) and its applications in analyzing complex astronomical data, from distinguishing dark matter models to reconstructing supernova neutrino energy spectra. Currently, I am also developing a reinforcement learning-based robotic system for radio telescope scheduling.


I am actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities to further explore how machine learning can unveil fundamental physical laws from vast astrophysical datasets.
